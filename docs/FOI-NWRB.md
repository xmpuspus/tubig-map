# FOI request to NWRB, ready to file

Status: drafted 2026-07-20, not yet filed. Xavier files this under his own
eGovPH / eFOI account. Nothing here is submitted automatically.

File at <https://www.foi.gov.ph> against agency **National Water Resources
Board (NWRB)**, legal basis Executive Order No. 2, s. 2016.

## Why this request exists

The map at <https://tubig-map.vercel.app> can show where golf courses and data
centers sit relative to the deep-well moratorium areas, and it can measure
relative greenness from satellite. It cannot show how much water either
industry is permitted to draw, because that is the one dataset nobody has
published. The two official golf rates in circulation conflict by one to two
orders of magnitude, and exactly one of fourteen tracked data center sites has
published any water metric. Permit-level data settles it.

NWRB Resolution No. 05-0925 (September 2025) required telemetered meters on
industrial and municipal permits at or above 10 L/s, with a 31 December 2025
deadline. As of 20 July 2026 no compliance data, audit result or dataset has
been published. Request item 4 asks directly about that.

## Suggested title

Water permit records and telemetry compliance for industrial, commercial and
recreational users in the deep-well moratorium areas

## Request body (paste as-is)

Under Executive Order No. 2, s. 2016, I request the following records held by
the National Water Resources Board. I am requesting these in machine-readable
form (CSV or XLSX preferred; a PDF export is acceptable if that is the only
format available).

1. A list of all active water permits in the National Capital Region, Bulacan,
   Cavite, Rizal and Laguna, with the following fields per permit:
   permit number; permit holder name; purpose or use category as recorded by
   NWRB; source type (groundwater or surface water); municipality or city;
   authorised abstraction rate in litres per second and, where recorded, the
   authorised annual volume in cubic meters; date granted; and current status.

2. The same list filtered to permits whose recorded purpose or use category is
   recreational, commercial, industrial, or any category NWRB uses to classify
   golf courses, resorts, or data centers and other information technology
   facilities. If NWRB does not have a distinct category for data centers,
   please state which category such facilities are recorded under.

3. For the five areas named in item 1, the total number of deep-well permit
   applications received, granted, denied and pending for each year from 2019
   to date, broken down by use category. If a moratorium on new deep wells is
   in force in any of these areas, please provide the issuance that establishes
   it, its coverage and its current status, together with any exemptions
   granted and the basis for each.

4. Records relating to NWRB Resolution No. 05-0925 (2025), which requires
   telemetered water meters for industrial and municipal permits with an
   authorised rate at or above 10 litres per second:
   a. the number of permits covered by the requirement, by region and use
      category;
   b. the number that had installed compliant telemetry as of the 31 December
      2025 deadline and as of the date this request is processed;
   c. any telemetry readings or abstraction data received to date, or a
      statement that none have been received;
   d. any enforcement, extension, or suspension action taken in relation to
      the requirement.

5. Any water permit records associated with the following facilities or their
   operators, or a statement that no such permit exists: VITRO data centers
   operated by ePLDT, including the planned facility in Trece Martires, Cavite;
   STT GDC Philippines facilities, including Fairview; Digital Edge NARRA1;
   YCO Cloud Centers; and Digital Halo.

6. Any water-conservation directive, advisory or notice issued by NWRB from
   2023 to date that names specific permit holders or facilities, together with
   the criteria used to select the facilities named.

If any part of this request is denied, please cite the specific exception
relied upon and release the remainder. If personal information of natural
persons is a concern, I am content to receive the records with individual
names redacted; the permit holder names I am seeking are those of companies,
clubs and institutions. If the volume of records makes any item burdensome,
please contact me and I will narrow it rather than have the request denied.

## What each item is for

| Item | What it settles |
|---|---|
| 1 | The denominator. Total permitted abstraction on restricted ground. |
| 2 | The actual golf-versus-data-center comparison, by permit rather than by conflicting press estimates. |
| 3 | Whether the moratorium is binding in practice or routinely excepted. |
| 4 | Whether the telemetry rule produced any data at all. This is the highest-value item. |
| 5 | Named-facility permits for the sites already on the map. |
| 6 | Whether the regulatory asymmetry the map documents shows up in NWRB's own selection criteria. |
