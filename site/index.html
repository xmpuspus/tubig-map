Who competes for Metro Manila's groundwater

Golf courses and the data center buildout draw on the same stressed groundwater, and some of both sit inside the areas where the National Water Resources Board banned new deep wells. Regulators have named golf courses in water-conservation directives repeatedly. They have never named a data center. This map shows both industries on the restricted ground, and reports what a Sentinel-2 measurement of the 2024 El Nino can and cannot support. Tested against a no-drought control season, the per-course stay-green signal failed. What survives is a population-level result and a baseline contrast, both reported below with the failure that produced them.

Why this is live right now

Angat Dam, which supplies about 90 percent of Metro Manila's raw water, is at its lowest recorded level. It fell to 152.85 m, which is 7.15 m below the 160 m critical level and 27.15 m below the 180 m minimum operating level. The NWRB cut the MWSS water allocation from 48 to 46 cubic meters per second for July 16 to 30, 2026. PAGASA has raised an El Nino Alert, with a 79 percent chance of El Nino developing over June to August 2026 and persisting into early 2027, which puts the Feb-Apr 2027 dry season at risk.

The satellite measurement on this page is not a picture of that crisis. The imagery windows are Feb-Apr 2024 (the last El Nino) and Feb-Apr 2026 (a normal season, after La Nina ended on 9 March 2026). They show how this competition behaved the last time water got scarce, which is why they matter as the next dry season approaches. They do not show July 2026.

Angat level and allocation cut: Philstar and BusinessWorld, July 2026. ENSO status: PAGASA advisories and NOAA CPC, July 2026. Full links in docs/SOURCES.md.

What the map shows

The regulatory asymmetry

In May 2024, at the peak of El Nino, the DENR ordered 13 named golf courses in Metro Manila and Rizal to conserve water: monitor mains overnight, prefer recycled water, stop irrigating from piped supply. No Philippine regulator has ever issued a water directive naming a data center. Scrutiny of the data center buildout has been about power, not water.

DENR directive: GMA News, May 2024. Power coverage: PCIJ, Jan 2026.

Same restricted ground

NWRB Resolution 001-0904 bans deep-well drilling in Metro Manila and in five named municipalities in Bulacan and Cavite. mapped golf courses covering hectares sit inside those named areas, alongside of the tracked data center sites. Counting Rizal, where an extension is reported but unconfirmed, raises that to courses and sites.

Resolution 001-0904 as quoted in Supreme Court G.R. No. 208383 (2016); boundaries from OSM. The five-province version this map used until 2026-07-20 was not supported by any source and roughly doubled these counts.

Nobody can compute the comparison

The two official golf water figures conflict by one to two orders of magnitude: an NWRB estimate cited in 2010 implies roughly 2,600 m3/day for a 50-hectare course, while MWSS in 2023 described 700 to 1,400 m3 per month per course. For data centers it is worse: of tracked sites, exactly operator has published any water metric. This map measures what can be measured and labels the rest.

IBON/NWRB 2010; MWSS via Philstar 2023; operator disclosures.

The satellite measurement

For each of the mapped golf courses, Sentinel-2 imagery compares greenness inside the course against a 300-meter ring around it, across three windows: 2019-2023 pooled, the 2024 El Nino, and ENSO-neutral 2026. The drought median rests on about cloud-free observations per pixel against for the pooled baseline, and courses have fewer than ten.

That thin single-season sampling is why the per-course drought signal does not survive its control. This measures greenness, not liters, and carries no accusation.

Method: NDVI medians, Cloud Score+ mask, Feb-Apr windows, per-course observation counts in data/ndvi_quality.csv.

What the measurement found, including where it failed

Group differences are permutation-tested (20,000 resamples, so p values floor at about 5 in 100,000 and are reported as bounds below that). Courses cluster spatially: 138 polygons form roughly 60 independent groups at 10 km, so tests here shuffle whole clusters where that matters and the effective sample size is nearer 60 to 99 than 138. This project ran 21 tests in total; at that family size a Bonferroni threshold is p = 0.0024, and each card below says whether it clears it. The first card governs how much weight the rest can carry.

The per-course detector failed its control

Feb-Apr 2026 was ENSO-neutral, so running the identical statistic on it gives a control with no drought to detect. If the threshold measured drought irrigation it should almost never fire there. It fires percent of the time in the normal season against percent in the drought season, and the spread of the two distributions is the same to within 3 percent.

So no individual course can be picked out of the noise, and this project does not claim otherwise. An earlier version of this page led with "28 courses stayed green", which is the drought-season count with no control beside it. That framing is withdrawn. The ranked table further down is kept only so the failure is visible: each course is shown in both seasons, and the two columns disagree about as often as they agree.

And it is not the wrong index. The obvious objection is that NDVI measures greenness, while water stress shows up first in canopy moisture. So the same measurement was re-run on NDMI, which uses the shortwave-infrared band where leaf water absorbs, from the same Sentinel-2 collection that was being queried all along. NDMI fails the same control: percent of courses clear the threshold in the drought season against percent with no drought, an excess of points against for NDVI. The two indices agree with each other (Pearson +0.88, 12 of 15 top courses shared), so they are measuring the same thing and neither can resolve one dry season of per-course irrigation response against a 300 m ring.

Nor is it the wrong physics. Reflectance indices read colour. The textbook way to see irrigation is heat: water leaving a wet canopy carries heat with it, so watered turf runs cooler than dry ground beside it. Landsat surface temperature has been free in the same archive throughout. It fails the control too, at percent in the drought against percent with none. Three instruments on three different physical channels, all unable to pick out a single course.

The population did respond to the drought

The same data supports a result about all courses together that it cannot support about any one of them. Mean signal was in the drought season against in the normal season, a paired shift of (permutation p = 0.002). Philippine golf courses as a class browned relative to their surroundings during the drought and returned to parity afterwards.

It replicates in a second drought. Measuring each baseline year separately, the average course-minus-ring gap was in 2019, when El Nino conditions carried into the dry season, and in the 2024 drought, against in the La Nina seasons of 2021 and 2022. Two independent droughts, nearly the same number, and the wet years clearly apart from both. That is the population effect showing up twice without being asked to.

And a second instrument agrees. Landsat surface temperature is a different physical channel from reflectance, and it tells the same story: courses sat K cooler than their surroundings in normal seasons and K warmer during the drought, a shift of K (p = 0.004). Browner and hotter than their neighbourhoods when water got scarce. The two instruments correlate at -0.61 across courses, which is the sign the physics requires.

That is the opposite direction from the viral version of this argument, and it is the most defensible thing in the dataset because averaging courses is exactly what beats the per-course noise. Worth knowing how it decomposes: into the drought the average course fell 0.036 in NDVI while its ring fell 0.021, so the courses browned harder than their surroundings. And 45 of 138 rings actually greened during the drought, which is a reminder that a ring is a piece of countryside with its own weather and crops, not a null.

The named courses are the most conspicuously green

This one is measured over five pooled seasons rather than one, so it rests on a median of observations per pixel instead of , and it survives where the single-season signal does not. In normal years the DENR-named courses stand out against their surroundings by an NDVI gap of , against for every other mapped course. Shuffling whole 10 km clusters rather than individual courses, so that neighbouring courses cannot count as independent evidence, gives p = 0.005. It is the only group comparison here that clears a Bonferroni threshold for the full family of 21 tests this project ran.

This contrast is chronic rather than seasonal: it correlates at +0.88 between the drought season and the normal one, so it describes how a course sits against its neighbourhood in general and not how it behaved when water got scarce. Much of it is simply where they sit: Metro Manila courses average +0.308 against +0.051 elsewhere, because the surroundings are built up. A persistent greenness contrast is equally consistent with heavier year-round irrigation and with being green land in a dense city, and this measurement cannot separate the two. It takes no view on whether the 13 were the right 13.

Forty-four courses are barer than their surroundings

In normal dry seasons of the mapped polygons have a negative baseline gap: the land inside the course is less green than the ring around it. A working irrigated course is not usually barer than its neighbourhood, so these are likely construction, sand-heavy layouts, closed courses, or OSM polygons that do not match the playing surface.

They matter because a course with an unusually bare baseline produces a large positive "signal" simply by returning to normal. They are excluded from the ranked table for that reason, which is why the previously top-ranked entry no longer appears.

The areas NWRB actually named

An earlier version of this map drew five whole provinces. The only source for that was a law firm article saying restrictions apply "especially in Metro Manila and nearby provinces (e.g., Bulacan, Cavite, Rizal, Laguna)", which names no resolution and claims no province-wide coverage. The Supreme Court, quoting NWRB Resolution 001-0904 in G.R. No. 208383 (2016), names Metro Manila plus Guiguinto, Bocaue, Marilao and Meycauayan in Bulacan and Dasmarinas in Cavite. Rizal is carried separately because a later extension is reported but the amending resolution could not be located. Laguna is dropped: no primary source names it. Correcting this roughly halved every co-location count on this page.

Honest limits

OSM is incomplete

Valley Golf and Country Club, one of the 13 DENR-named courses, has no polygon in OpenStreetMap, so it is absent here. Course counts for the country range from 75 to 175 depending on source; no census exists.

Greenness is not volume

A strong stay-green signal is consistent with irrigation, including irrigation from recycled or harvested water, which is exactly what the DENR directive asks for. The signal cannot say where the water came from.

Data center pins vary in precision

Each site carries a precision label and its source. of are building-precision; the rest are city, district or campus centroids, so this map makes no claim about how physically close any data center is to any golf course. Co-location is stated at the level of the named restricted area only. Capacity claims for the national buildout disagree by an order of magnitude across 2025-2026 sources; megawatt figures carry their source.

One threshold, and it did not work

The cutoff was chosen, not discovered: there is no natural break in the distribution. Tested against the no-drought 2026 season it fires more often with no drought than with one, so it is reported here as a failed instrument rather than used to count courses. Every underlying value is in the repo.

OSM polygons overlap and repeat

polygons lie wholly inside another mapped course, and several courses are split across multiple polygons. Nested polygons are flagged, excluded from totals and from the table, and left visible on the map. Totals count standalone polygons.

Many things other than irrigation move this number

A course can read greener than its ring because it has mature tree canopy while the ring is bare, because it contains lakes or ponds, because it sits on a high water table or a spring, because rain fell unevenly across 300 metres, or because turf was relaid that year. A course can read browner because the ring is irrigated rice paddy on its own planting calendar. Twenty-three of the 85 larger courses have rings greener than the course itself. None of these can be separated from irrigation by an optical index, and staying green is in any case compatible with the DENR directive, which asks courses to switch to recycled water rather than to stop watering.

Most of the map is not Metro Manila

The headline asks about Metro Manila's groundwater, but the golf layer is national: only of mapped courses are inside an area NWRB named, and several courses in the table are on other islands entirely. Rows are labelled with their area so this is visible rather than implied.

The error bars are wide and incomplete

Intervals come from repeat-observation scatter only. They do not cover geolocation error, atmospheric correction residual, or the fact that a control ring is not matched to its course on land cover, soil or slope. A ring dominated by irrigated rice paddy carries its own water signal. Median interval width is times four, which is wider than almost every signal on the page.

If something here is wrong about your organisation

Every figure on this page is computed from public data by scripts in the repository, and every course and site is identifiable, so errors here attach to real named organisations. If a polygon is not your land, a name is wrong, a status is out of date, a water disclosure exists that this project did not find, or a measurement looks wrong to you, the fastest route is to open an issue or email xpuspus@gmail.com.

Corrections are applied to the underlying data and the change is recorded in docs/DOUBT-LOOP.md with what was wrong and what replaced it, the same way this project has recorded its own withdrawn findings. Corrections that arrive with evidence do not need to argue for themselves.
