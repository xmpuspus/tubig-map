Why this is live right now

Angat Dam, which supplies about 90 percent of Metro Manila's raw water, is at its lowest recorded level. It fell to 152.85 m, which is 7.15 m below the 160 m critical level and 27.15 m below the 180 m minimum operating level. The NWRB cut the MWSS water allocation from 48 to 46 cubic meters per second for July 16 to 30, 2026. PAGASA has raised an El Nino Alert, with a 79 percent chance of El Nino developing over June to August 2026 and persisting into early 2027, which puts the Feb-Apr 2027 dry season at risk.

The satellite measurement on this page is not a picture of that crisis. The imagery windows are Feb-Apr 2024 (the last El Nino) and Feb-Apr 2026 (a normal season, after La Nina ended on 9 March 2026). They show how this competition behaved the last time water got scarce, which is why they matter as the next dry season approaches. They do not show July 2026.

Angat level and allocation cut: Philstar and BusinessWorld, July 2026. ENSO status: PAGASA advisories and NOAA CPC, July 2026. Full links in docs/SOURCES.md.

What the map shows

The regulatory asymmetry: versus

In May 2024, at the peak of El Nino, the DENR ordered 13 named golf courses in Metro Manila and Rizal to conserve water: monitor mains overnight, prefer recycled water, stop irrigating from piped supply. No Philippine regulator has ever issued a water directive naming a data center. Scrutiny of the data center buildout has been about power, not water.

DENR directive: GMA News, May 2024. Power coverage: PCIJ, Jan 2026.

Same restricted ground

NWRB Resolution 001-0904 (2004) designates eight critical groundwater areas covering sixteen local government units in Bulacan, Metro Manila and Cavite, and restricts how water permits are issued there. mapped golf courses covering hectares sit inside them, alongside of the tracked data center sites. Counting the wider extent the Supreme Court and 2008 press describe for a later ban, all of Metro Manila plus Rizal, raises that to courses and sites.

Primary document: NWRB Res. 001-0904, 22 September 2004, retrieved via the Internet Archive. Boundaries from OSM. This map said "five provinces" until 2026-07-20 and then "Metro Manila entire" until later the same day; both overstated the designation, and the counts have fallen by more than half since.

Nobody can compute the comparison

The two official golf water figures conflict by one to two orders of magnitude: an NWRB estimate cited in 2010 implies roughly 2,600 m3/day for a 50-hectare course, while MWSS in 2023 described 700 to 1,400 m3 per month per course. For data centers it is worse: of tracked sites, exactly operator has published any water metric. This map measures what can be measured and labels the rest.

IBON/NWRB 2010; MWSS via Philstar 2023; operator disclosures.

The satellite measurement

For each of the mapped golf courses, Sentinel-2 imagery compares greenness inside the course against a 300-meter ring around it, across three windows: 2019-2023 pooled, the 2024 El Nino, and ENSO-neutral 2026. The drought median rests on about cloud-free observations per pixel against for the pooled baseline, and courses have fewer than ten.

That thin single-season sampling is why the per-course drought signal does not survive its control. This measures greenness, not liters, and carries no accusation.

Method: NDVI medians, Cloud Score+ mask, Feb-Apr windows, per-course observation counts in data/ndvi_quality.csv.

What the measurement found

The DENR-named courses, mostly explained

In normal seasons the named courses stand out against their surroundings by more than every other course (cluster p = 0.005, the largest effect measured here). Enter the ring's built-up fraction and that falls to : two thirds of it was the fact that those courses sit in dense Metro Manila and the others do not. What is left is small and this measurement cannot say whether it is irrigation.

The thermal result, and why it does not rescue anything

Landsat surface temperature moved K against the same 2026 comparator (cluster p = 0.022), courses running hotter relative to their surroundings in the drought. It was published as independent corroboration. It is differenced against the same ring, so it inherits the same confound: roofs heat differently from grass. Measured from the pooled baseline instead, the move is 0.114 K and not significant (cluster p = 0.35). Kept here rather than deleted, because it was on this page and readers saw it.

What is left standing

One satellite result, properly controlled: golf turf is NDVI greener than nearby grassland in normal dry seasons. Managed turf beats unmanaged grass, which is what management looks like and is not a water volume. Then the regulatory asymmetry: golf courses named in a Philippine water directive against data centers. Public record, no satellite involved. And the volume comparison remains uncomputable: the two official golf rates differ by two orders of magnitude and of data center sites publish a water metric.

What is withdrawn

Any ranking of courses by drought response, withdrawn in round 1 after the detector fired more often with no drought than with one. And now the two population findings that were differenced against the ring, because that difference is largely land cover. This page has published four instrument failures and two withdrawn findings; none of it is hidden.

Where a real measurement would start

Both grass controls here are still opportunistic: whatever grass happens to sit nearby, not grass matched to a course on soil, slope, aspect and rainfall. The two frames correlate only +0.27, which is how much that matters. A propensity-matched design, or metre-scale imagery that separates fairway from rough and tree from turf, is what would settle the magnitude. Neither is free.

The areas NWRB actually designated

This took three attempts and the record is worth stating. The map first drew five whole provinces, sourced from a law firm article saying restrictions apply "especially in Metro Manila and nearby provinces (e.g., Bulacan, Cavite, Rizal, Laguna)", which names no resolution. It then drew Metro Manila entire, from the Supreme Court's description in G.R. No. 208383. The primary document, obtained from the Internet Archive after nwrb.gov.ph refused automated requests, is narrower than both: Resolution 001-0904 designates eight critical areas, and they are sub-city. Manila, Malabon, Marikina, San Juan, Taguig and Valenzuela are not among them. Two entries are partial, since "North Caloocan" and "West Quezon City" have no administrative boundary to draw and the whole city is shown instead. Laguna appears in no source and is dropped. Metro Manila entire and Rizal are kept as the wider extent later reporting describes, labelled as such.

Honest limits

OSM is incomplete

Valley Golf and Country Club, one of the 13 DENR-named courses, has no polygon in OpenStreetMap, so it is absent here. Course counts for the country range from 75 to 175 depending on source; no census exists.

Greenness is not volume

A strong stay-green signal is consistent with irrigation, including irrigation from recycled or harvested water, which is exactly what the DENR directive asks for. The signal cannot say where the water came from.

Data center pins vary in precision

Each site carries a precision label and its source. of are building-precision; the rest are city, district or campus centroids, so this map makes no claim about how physically close any data center is to any golf course. Co-location is stated at the level of the named restricted area only. Capacity claims for the national buildout disagree by an order of magnitude across 2025-2026 sources; megawatt figures carry their source.

One threshold, and it did not work

The cutoff was chosen, not discovered: there is no natural break in the distribution. Tested against the no-drought 2026 season it fires more often with no drought than with one, so it is reported here as a failed instrument rather than used to count courses. Every underlying value is in the repo.

OSM polygons overlap and repeat

polygons lie wholly inside another mapped course, and several courses are split across multiple polygons. Nested polygons are flagged, excluded from totals and from the table, and left visible on the map. Totals count standalone polygons.

Many things other than irrigation move this number

A course can read greener than its ring because it has mature tree canopy while the ring is bare, because it contains lakes or ponds, because it sits on a high water table or a spring, because rain fell unevenly across 300 metres, or because turf was relaid that year. A course can read browner because the ring is irrigated rice paddy on its own planting calendar. Twenty-three of the 85 larger courses have rings greener than the course itself. None of these can be separated from irrigation by an optical index, and staying green is in any case compatible with the DENR directive, which asks courses to switch to recycled water rather than to stop watering.

Most of the map is not Metro Manila

The headline asks about Metro Manila's groundwater, but the golf layer is national: only of mapped courses are inside an area NWRB named, and several courses in the table are on other islands entirely. Rows are labelled with their area so this is visible rather than implied.

The error bars are wide and incomplete

Intervals come from repeat-observation scatter only. They do not cover geolocation error, atmospheric correction residual, or the fact that a control ring is not matched to its course on land cover, soil or slope. A ring dominated by irrigated rice paddy carries its own water signal. Median interval width is times four, which is wider than almost every signal on the page.

If something here is wrong about your organisation

Every figure on this page is computed from public data by scripts in the repository, and every course and site is identifiable, so errors here attach to real named organisations. If a polygon is not your land, a name is wrong, a status is out of date, a water disclosure exists that this project did not find, or a measurement looks wrong to you, the fastest route is to open an issue or email xpuspus@gmail.com.

Corrections are applied to the underlying data and the change is recorded in docs/DOUBT-LOOP.md with what was wrong and what replaced it, the same way this project has recorded its own withdrawn findings. Corrections that arrive with evidence do not need to argue for themselves.
