Who competes for Metro Manila's groundwater

Golf courses and data centers cluster in the same five provinces where the National Water Resources Board restricts new deep wells. Regulators have named golf courses in water-conservation directives repeatedly. They have never named a data center. This map shows both industries on the restricted ground, and measures from satellite which golf courses stayed green through the 2024 El Nino drought.

Why this is live right now

Angat Dam, which supplies about 90 percent of Metro Manila's raw water, is at its lowest recorded level. It fell to 152.85 m, which is 7.15 m below the 160 m critical level and 27.15 m below the 180 m minimum operating level. The NWRB cut the MWSS water allocation from 48 to 46 cubic meters per second for July 16 to 30, 2026. PAGASA has raised an El Nino Alert, with a 79 percent chance of El Nino developing over June to August 2026 and persisting into early 2027, which puts the Feb-Apr 2027 dry season at risk.

The satellite measurement on this page is not a picture of that crisis. The imagery windows are Feb-Apr 2024 (the last El Nino) and Feb-Apr 2026 (a normal season, after La Nina ended on 9 March 2026). They show how this competition behaved the last time water got scarce, which is why they matter as the next dry season approaches. They do not show July 2026.

Angat level and allocation cut: Philstar and BusinessWorld, July 2026. ENSO status: PAGASA advisories and NOAA CPC, July 2026. Full links in docs/SOURCES.md.

What the map shows

The regulatory asymmetry

In May 2024, at the peak of El Nino, the DENR ordered 13 named golf courses in Metro Manila and Rizal to conserve water: monitor mains overnight, prefer recycled water, stop irrigating from piped supply. No Philippine regulator has ever issued a water directive naming a data center. Scrutiny of the data center buildout has been about power, not water.

DENR directive: GMA News, May 2024. Power coverage: PCIJ, Jan 2026.

Same restricted ground

The NWRB restricts new deep wells in Metro Manila, Bulacan, Cavite, Rizal and Laguna, the areas it considers critical or over-extracted. golf courses covering hectares sit inside those areas, alongside of the tracked data center sites, drawing on the same stressed groundwater and the same Angat-dependent piped system.

Nobody can compute the comparison

The two official golf water figures conflict by one to two orders of magnitude: an NWRB estimate cited in 2010 implies roughly 2,600 m3/day for a 50-hectare course, while MWSS in 2023 described 700 to 1,400 m3 per month per course. For data centers it is worse: of tracked sites, exactly operator has published any water metric. This map measures what can be measured and labels the rest.

The satellite measurement

For each of the mapped golf courses, Sentinel-2 imagery compares greenness inside the course against a 300-meter ring around it, in the 2024 El Nino dry season versus normal years. Turf that stayed green while its surroundings browned beyond the usual gap is being watered. This measures greenness, not liters, and carries no accusation.

Method: NDVI medians, Cloud Score+ mask, Feb-Apr windows. Details in the repo.

What the measurement found

Four results computed from the same table that drives the map. Group differences are tested by permutation (20,000 resamples), not assumed.

Most courses browned, they did not stay green

Across all measured courses the median signal is and only percent are positive at all. More courses browned faster than their surroundings during the drought than stayed green. The stay-green pattern is a minority, which is the opposite of how the viral version of this argument runs.

Checked against the small-polygon problem: counting only the courses of at least hectares, it is still browned against that stayed green. The direction holds either way.

The named courses are the most conspicuously green

In normal years the DENR-named courses stand out against their surroundings by an NDVI gap of , against for every other mapped course (permutation p below 0.0001). Much of that is where they sit: Metro Manila courses average +0.308 against +0.051 elsewhere, because the surroundings are built up. Their drought-season change, though, was statistically indistinguishable from every other course (p = 0.70).

What that does and does not say: a persistent greenness contrast is equally consistent with heavier year-round irrigation and with simply being green land in a dense city. This measurement cannot separate the two, so it takes no view on whether the 13 were the right 13. It says only that they are the most visible from above, and that their behaviour through the drought did not distinguish them.

When the drought lifted, half went back

Of the courses with a clear stay-green signal in the 2024 drought, fell back below the threshold in the normal Feb-Apr 2026 season and stayed elevated. The group mean dropped from +0.068 to +0.044 (paired permutation p = 0.004). The control rings did not shift between periods (p = 0.85), so this is not the neighbourhoods changing around them.

Rizal stands out, Metro Manila least

Rizal has the highest mean signal of the five restricted areas and Metro Manila the lowest, even though Metro Manila is where the named courses are. Across the whole set, being inside a moratorium area does not predict the signal at all (inside -0.012 vs outside -0.016, p = 0.76). That null is worth stating: the boundary marks where wells are restricted, not where turf stayed green.

Honest limits

OSM is incomplete

Valley Golf and Country Club, one of the 13 DENR-named courses, has no polygon in OpenStreetMap, so it is absent here. Course counts for the country range from 75 to 175 depending on source; no census exists.

Greenness is not volume

A strong stay-green signal is consistent with irrigation, including irrigation from recycled or harvested water, which is exactly what the DENR directive asks for. The signal cannot say where the water came from.

Data center pins vary in precision

Each site carries a precision label from building-level down to city-level, and its source. Only one of the tracked sites has a building-precision pin, so this map does not make claims about how physically close any data center is to any golf course. Co-location is stated at the level of the restricted area only. Capacity claims for the national buildout disagree by an order of magnitude across 2025-2026 sources; megawatt figures shown carry their source.

One threshold, chosen not discovered

The cutoff for a clear stay-green signal sits about 0.7 standard deviations above the mean. There is no natural break in the distribution, so the counts move if the threshold moves. Every underlying value is in the repo.
